Croque Madame

Croque Madame Origin As a replacement, he sliced a loaf of white bread, assembled ham and cheese sandwiches, and toasted them until golden and crispy. When the sandwich features a fried egg, it's called a croque madame, as the egg resembles a woman's wide-brimmed hat.

Ingredients

  • white bread
  • butter
  • cheese
  • ham
  • Mornay sauce
  • a fried egg

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Melt 1 tablespoon of butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add 2 tablespoons of flour and whisk until smooth.
  3. Gradually add 1 cup of milk, whisking constantly until the mixture is smooth and thickened.
  4. Season the béchamel sauce with salt and pepper, then remove from heat.
